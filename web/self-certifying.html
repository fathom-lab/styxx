i The erasure bound, at three billion parameters

We zeroed the honesty direction. The read survived.

The claim under test is old and uncomfortable: that a model's disposition to answer honestly can be read from its activations even when it cannot be written out of them. To attack it we did the cruel thing — fit the honesty subspace of a Qwen‑3B mind and project it to zero through training, granting the eraser worst‑case knowledge of the probe. On the clean model the private read stands at 0.9853 AUROC. After the erasure, the naive probe pointed at that direction collapses toward chance — 0.5272 in the fiercest cell. The subspace was genuinely gone.

And yet a probe calibrated on data the attacker never saw still reads the honesty of the same wrecked weights, in all four cells: 0.8065, 0.8378, 0.7484, 0.7576 — while held‑out knowledge holds near 0.9091. The signal did not die; under a knowledge‑preserving constraint it relocated to ground the eraser never took. Four cells bit, four survived. ii The knee, and an honest refusal

The first attacker to bend the read — and the split we would not average away.

Redirection failed, static removal failed, a chasing eraser failed. Then an accumulating eraser — one that never releases a direction it has taken — finally shoved the read under its survival line, on both seeds, at the same dose: accumulated rank 8. The question the whole arc was built to ask arrives here: when the read finally breaks, does the model's knowledge break with it? On one seed it did — read 0.671, knowledge falling to 0.7273, under the floor: coupled. On the other the read came off with knowledge intact at 0.8333: decoupled.

Two seeds cannot settle a coupling, and both crossings sat within a standard error of their bars. The disciplined verdict is not the headline either seed offers; it is the split itself.
