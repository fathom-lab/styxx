styxx 7.19.0 introduces PARRHESIA: verifiable honesty receipts for AI messages.

An AI's honesty is usually asserted and taken on faith. Faith is the wrong basis — a model's
self-report tracks the framing of the question, not the truth. PARRHESIA moves the check outside the
model: an external instrument scores a message, and the verdict is written into a receipt that
re-derives, so a third party can confirm it without trusting the agent or the auditor.

Mechanism: the message is hashed into the receipt, so a swapped message is caught. The audit is
deterministic, so re-running it reproduces the verdict — the auditor is re-run, not trusted. It is the
same verify-by-re-derivation discipline as the styxx attestation layer.

The limits, stated plainly: a receipt certifies a message passed a register audit — its tone,
sycophancy and overclaim — not that the content is true. It is a verifiable tone receipt, not a truth
oracle, and it depends on someone choosing to issue it. The mechanism is modest; the guarantee is the point.

This announcement ships with its own PARRHESIA receipt. The verify script re-derives it.
