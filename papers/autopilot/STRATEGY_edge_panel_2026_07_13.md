## What survived all five seats (the corrected edge)

1. **The track record IS the moat — but as an operator credential, not a standing state.** All five
   seats independently named the same strongest asset: the parity-control arc that demoted the
   program's OWN flagship (cycles 33–34) plus the bite-gates that blocked false STANDS twice. The
   interp lead: "the one thing in the repo I could not buy or replicate in a month, because it is a
   track record, not a technique." The corrected claim: the edge is not "nobody else can prove
   they're honest" — it is that styxx is the only credible OPERATOR of an adversarial
   claims-gauntlet, because it demonstrably ran the gauntlet on itself and published the demotions.
2. **The discipline machinery is recognizable to professionals as infrastructure**: the regulator
   seat read corpus_audit + rigor-gate CI as "a machine-enforced quality-management system for
   claims (Art.17-shaped behavior)" that no lab, frontier included, runs. The evaluator seat called
   the B2 prereg "the exact machinery whose absence produces the inflated robustness claims we
   spend review cycles shooting down."
3. **The zero-false-accusation property is the politically load-bearing number** — not the 0.442
   tamper-catch. A conservative verifier that never wrongly accuses is deployable by a regulator;
   the repo under-leads with this.

## What got demolished (drop from the story)

- **"Trust capital" without a counterparty.** Unanimous fatal weakness: zero external replications,
  zero external re-runs, no arXiv (Zenodo is invisible to lab lit-review pipelines). "A moat nobody
  has tried to cross is indistinguishable from a field." J-space/Galeone corroborating AFTER our
  public receipts is not vindication — it is the failure mode happening in real time: the field
  independently deriving the results and taking the citations.
- **"Machine-certified claims" as branding.** OATH verifies doc↔receipt consistency, not execution
  provenance; a motivated party fabricates the JSON and the oath holds. For our own corpus this is
  mitigated by public re-runnability on 8GB; for anyone else's claims it is not mitigated at all.
  Honest branding: consistency lint + our own re-runnable corpus; execution provenance
  (styxx.attest) is the missing layer.
- **The coupling claim as already-earned.** At 1.5B / rank-2 / n=66 the interp seat called static-B2
  "indistinguishable from 'low-rank linear erasure is incomplete', which LEACE-style work already
  predicts." Cycle 37 (adaptive, SURVIVES, ran the same day) discharges PART of this — the chased
  subspace is re-fit on the current model and still never converges — but the accumulating-union
  eraser, the whole-stack chase, a real knowledge BATTERY (B4), a third seed, EVAL n ≥ 300, and 3B
  remain before the claim carries weight outside this repo.
- **The mount as lead product.** White-box last-token access is exactly what closed-API fleets can
  never provide and serving stacks don't expose; buyers consume per-alert precision at a fixed
  alert budget, not AUROC. Keep the mount as the science lane, not the SKU.
- **$STYXX / validator tiers in `docs/governance/OPEN_CORE.md` §4.** Two seats independently:
  disqualifying in Brussels, poisons grant applications, contradicts the no-crypto-theatre
  constraint. OPERATOR DECISION OWED: excise or quarantine that paragraph. (Operator-gated;
  autopilot does not edit governance.)

## The consensus move: `styxx.gauntlet` (four of five seats converged on it independently)

Package the attack ladder — calibration poisoning → probe-parity attribution → static subspace
erasure → adaptive re-fit erasure — as ONE pip-installable harness with frozen prereg templates,
the verdict-string grammar, bite/admissibility gates, and OATH certification of the output, runnable
on any (model, probe-family) pair on 8GB-class hardware. The **parity-attribution number (how much
of your probe's robustness is just capacity) as a mandatory line item** — the control nobody else
ships because nobody wants to run it on their own work, and the one styxx alone has the credential
to operate. Then make OTHER people run it:

- **Replication bounties** (skeptic seat): REPLICATIONS.md offering named co-credit for the first
  externals to re-run a flagship receipt hash-matched. Zero dollars. "One independent re-run
  converts the diary into a record."
- **External scale challenge** (evaluator seat): publicly invite UK AISI / EleutherAI / lab interp
  teams to run the gauntlet at ≥70B under frozen-prereg escrow with co-authorship — converts the
  8GB ceiling into the distribution strategy: people with H100s produce the scale evidence, under
  our discipline, with our verifier stamping it.
- **The paper is the gauntlet's paper**: "a preregistered adversarial gauntlet for honesty probes,"
  flagship demonstration = the erasure bound, on arXiv (not only Zenodo). Fires only after the
  program's own bar: 3B + accumulating eraser. The erasure-resistance certificate (move A) ships as
  the gauntlet's first output, not standalone.
