## what this does and does not show

It shows the full protocol runs end-to-end on a frontier-model panel with the audit blind to
truth, and that in the easy regime the instrument neither invents sync, nor flags phantom
misfit, nor refuses good data -- the exact behavior the sealed datasheet promised for clean
panels. It also shows the operator's directive answered honestly: pointed at its maker's
family, the instrument found nothing to accuse, and said so with calibrated machinery rather
than flattery.

It does NOT stress the instrument on this family, because the corpus cannot stress the family:
a task ladder calibrated to trouble 3B-parameter judges is decidable at ceiling by the audited
system (compare rung 1, where the same corpus drives Qwen2.5-3B personas to organic
false-fire rates the anchors cannot see). A Claude-stressing rung needs a genuinely hard
ladder -- ambiguous entailment, long-document consistency, adversarially near-consistent
contradictions -- and replicates. Until then, the only claims made here are the ones above,
and none of them transfer past this corpus.

One observation worth carrying: the three-way byte-identity means persona diversity is NOT
error diversity for a strong model on a decidable task. Panels of one frontier model in
different costumes are one judge. The anchored instrument is built for exactly that world --
it never assumed the costumes were different people.
