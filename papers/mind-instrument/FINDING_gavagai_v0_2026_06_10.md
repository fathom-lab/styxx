# FINDING — radical translation between artificial minds is possible (TRANSLATION-POSSIBLE)

**2026-06-10 · Fathom Lab / styxx. Pre-registered: `PREREG_gavagai_v0_2026_06_10.md` (frozen
pre-run). Receipt: `gavagai_v0_result.json`. Local, $0. Both frozen gates passed.**

## The question

Quine (1960): an interpreter with no shared language, no paired examples, and no ostension cannot
determine what a foreign mind's terms refer to — reference is radically indeterminate. Harnad
(1990): symbols cannot carry meaning without grounding outside the symbol system. Both stood for
decades as philosophy because no one could read two independent minds. Here, ten artificial minds
(six convergence anchors + gpt2, gpt2-large, pythia-410m, Qwen2.5-0.5B), norm-equalized geometry
(`FINDING_anatomy_v2_2026_06_10.md`), and a label-free translator: sorted-distance signatures,
Hungarian assignment, mapped-RDM refinement. The foreign mind's 96 battery concepts are presented
in seeded-random order; the translator sees geometry only.

## Result

- **G1 PASSED — translation exists.** Mean cross-family concept-identification accuracy 0.1661
  against chance 0.0104 (sixteen times chance; frozen bar: ten times) and an identity-decoupled
  null whose 95th percentile is 0.0312 (100 runs). Self-pair pipeline control: 1.0.
- **G3 PASSED — kind-translation is stronger.** Mean cross-family category accuracy 0.3826 against
  chance 0.125 (bar 0.25).
- **Range:** best cross-family pairs translate far above the mean — Llama-3.2-3B→gemma-2-2b 0.385,
  Llama-3.2-1B→gemma-2-2b 0.375, Qwen2.5-3B→gemma-2-2b 0.344, and gpt2→pythia-410m 0.302
  (different labs, eras, and data). Within-family reference: Llama-3.2-1B→Llama-3.2-3B 0.510.
- **G2 — the apparatus discovery is what unlocked it:** under the old norm-dominated convention the
  same translator achieves only 0.0437; norm-equalization adds +0.1224 mean accuracy. The bare-word
  artifact was not just suppressing measured convergence — it was suppressing *recoverable
  identity*.

## What this establishes

In the first setting where Quine's thesis could be quantitatively tested, the strong reading fails:
**reference is substantially recoverable from relational structure alone** — no shared language, no
shared training, no grounding channel between the minds. Concept identity flows through pure
second-order geometry at sixteen times chance, concept kind at three times chance. The convergent
geometry of meaning is not merely similar across independent minds; it is similar in a way that
CARRIES IDENTITY — an interlingua in the literal sense. The symbol-grounding edge: identity
transferred between two systems neither of which has any grounding outside text.

## What this does not establish

Translation is partial (one concept in six, not six in six); the battery is 96 concrete nouns in 8
categories — abstract meaning untested; both minds were measured by the same instrument convention
(shared-apparatus structure is controlled by the identity-decoupled null but not by a second
modality); and Quine's thesis concerned human languages and behavioral evidence — this is its
in-silico structural analogue, not its ethnographic original. Scaling the translator (richer
signatures, larger batteries, anchor-free populations) is the obvious rung: if accuracy climbs with
battery size and structure richness, "indeterminacy of translation" becomes a measurable, shrinking
quantity rather than a principle.
