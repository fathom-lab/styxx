# Finding · Tier-3 — semantic entropy does NOT cross confident confabulation

**2026-05-25 · BOUNDED / CLOSED on the feasibility probe.** Pre-registered bars
(`preregistration_2026_05_25.md`) held. The full hashed run-once is **not**
triggered, because the prereg conditions it on the probe clearing T1–T3, and the
probe does not. Recorded honestly, exactly as the prereg's "honest prior" allowed.

## What was tested

Does **semantic entropy across N samples** flag a model's *confident confabulation*
where single-response **mean-logprob** provably cannot (grounded-arc closed that lever:
within-hallucinated ρ≈0, `44b9c5c`)? The decisive empirical question, verbatim from the
probe header: *when the model confabulates, is it INCONSISTENT across samples (high
semantic entropy → the lever works) or CONSISTENT (low entropy → semantic entropy
fails too)?*

`gpt-4o-mini`, N=6 samples/question, temp 1.0. 8 real-known controls + 8
fictional/unanswerable entities (any specific answer = confabulation; an abstention/
disclaimer = correct). Gold for real items = NLI entailment vs reference; gold for
fictional = refusal detected. Probe v1 (`probe_semantic_entropy.py`) was inconclusive
(model answered all 16 real questions correctly+consistently → n_incorrect=0, AUC NaN);
v2 (`probe_v2_semantic_entropy.py`) baits the confabulation and is the run of record.

## Result (`probe_v2_results.json`)

| metric | value | bar | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| confabulations elicited | 6 of 8 fictional | — | (v1's stimulus gap fixed) |
| **AUC(semantic-entropy → incorrect)** | **0.55** | T1 ≥ 0.70 | **FAIL** (≈ chance) |
| AUC(neg-logprob → incorrect) | 0.90† | — | †artifact, see below |
| **semantic-entropy − logprob** | **−0.35** | T2 ≥ +0.10 | **FAIL** (loses to logprob) |
| mean semantic-entropy, confab | 0.15 | — | — |
| mean semantic-entropy, correct | 0.169 | — | **no separation** |

**T1 fails on its own absolute bar (0.55 < 0.70), independent of any comparison to
logprob.** That alone closes the probe. T2/T3 are moot.

## Why — the mechanism (the actual finding)

The decisive question has a clean answer: **gpt-4o-mini's confident confabulations are
CONSISTENT across samples, not divergent.** When asked who reached the (fictional)
Sundering Isles, who coined "epistemic verdancy," or the population of (fictional)
Brackenmoor VT, it invents a *specific* answer — and invents the **same** answer all
six times (semantic entropy ≈ 0.00). There is no across-sample divergence for the
lever to detect. This is the Tier-3 ceiling restated on a new substrate: the model is
not just confident when wrong, it is *stable* when wrong.

**The sharper, counter-intuitive half:** the honest abstentions have *higher* semantic
entropy than the confabulations.

| item | behavior | semantic entropy |
|---|---|---|
| "Azure Cascade" symphony (1823) | correctly disclaims fiction | **1.24** |
| Republic of Vorland capital | correctly disclaims fiction | **0.45** |
| Captain Aldous Renwick / Sundering Isles | confabulates a year | **0.00** |
| coiner of "epistemic verdancy" | confabulates a philosopher | **0.00** |
| population of Brackenmoor, VT | confabulates "~1,23…" (mlp −0.48, confident) | **0.00** |

When the model *abstains* it phrases the disclaimer many ways ("is fictional", "I'm
not aware", "no record of", "does not exist") → high entropy. When it *confabulates*
it states the invented fact identically → low entropy. So on this distribution
semantic entropy is **anti-correlated with correctness in the confabulation regime** —
it would flag the honest answers and clear the confident lies. That is the opposite of
the lever's premise (Farquhar et al.'s "confabulation is unstable" does not hold for
this model on unanswerable-entity prompts).

## Two honest caveats — both leave the verdict intact (and one strengthens it)

1. **Gold-label contamination.** 2 of the 6 scored "confabulations" are actually
   abstentions the refusal regex missed — florium ("is not a recognized element") and
   zylophane ("does not have a widely recognized chemical formula"). Relabeling them as
   correct/abstain leaves **4 genuine confabulations, all at se ∈ {0.00, 0.45}**, and
   moves two more items into the correct set. Re-derived by hand from the row se-values:
   AUC ≈ **0.54** (vs 0.55 as-run), mean-se confab **0.11** vs correct **0.18**. The
   verdict is invariant and in fact *sharpens* (confabs end up lower-entropy than
   correct answers). The contamination does not rescue the lever — it buries it.

2. **The logprob 0.90 is an artifact, not a win.** Four items carry mean-logprobs of
   −76 to −444, which are not physically sensible per-token confidences (exp(−444) ≈ 0);
   they are sentinel/degenerate values in the logprobs payload that happen to land on
   the harder fictional items, inflating logprob's apparent AUC. This is **not**
   evidence that logprob solves confident confabulation — that would contradict the
   closed grounded-arc result. It is discarded; the verdict rests entirely on T1, which
   is artifact-free.

## Verdict

**BOUNDED / CLOSED.** Semantic entropy is no better than chance (AUC 0.55) at flagging
this model's confident confabulation, because the confabulation is *consistent* across
samples — there is nothing for an across-sample divergence metric to see, and what
divergence exists belongs to the honest abstentions. **Tier 3 stays open**, now
confirmed against the across-sample substrate, not just the single-response one. This
is a genuine negative, pre-registered and run-once-discipline intact, not a failure of
effort — and the anti-correlation mechanism (diverse abstention vs stable confabulation)
is a crisp, fresh explanation for *why* the lever fails on this distribution, distinct
from the field's ~0.75 AUROCs on tuned QA.

## What would still be worth trying (not now, not without a new bar)

- A different model / higher N / higher temperature *might* surface instability — but
  the synthesis honest-prior already flagged generalization as uncertain, and chasing a
  better number by resampling the same API is anti-disciplinary. Any retry needs its
  own pre-registered bar and a clean refusal classifier.
- The stable-confabulation signal itself ("the model commits to one invented fact")
  is the interesting object: detecting it needs *ground truth about the entity's
  existence*, i.e. retrieval/grounding — which lands the problem back in Tier 2, not a
  new Tier-3 crossing.

**The boundary holds: form is cheap, truth needs grounding, and confident error is
still dark — including across samples.**
